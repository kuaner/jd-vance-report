# 2028 Republican Presidential Primary: Competitors Intelligence Report

**Compiled**: 2026-03-24
**Focus**: All major potential GOP candidates, polling data, donor dynamics, and strategic positioning

---

## Executive Summary

The 2028 Republican primary is shaping up as a two-person race between Vice President **JD Vance** and Secretary of State **Marco Rubio**, with Rubio's stock surging dramatically since the Iran war began in late February 2026. Behind them, **Donald Trump Jr.** has emerged as a surprisingly strong third option, while **Ron DeSantis**, once seen as the inevitable Trump successor, has seen his prospects diminish significantly. **Nikki Haley** remains a distant possibility. **Vivek Ramaswamy** and **Byron Donalds** are focused on governor's races in Ohio and Florida respectively.

## 1. Marco Rubio -- The Ascendant Challenger

![Rubio at Iran strikes briefing](../assets/rubio-02.jpg)
![Rubio official portrait](../assets/rubio-01.jpg)

### Current Position
- **Title**: 72nd U.S. Secretary of State (sworn in Jan 21, 2025); also serving as acting National Security Adviser and acting USAID Director
- **Age**: 54 (born 1971, Miami, FL)
- **2028 Status**: Not declared, but "Draft Rubio" movement gaining momentum

### Key Developments (Feb-Mar 2026)

**Iran War Elevates Profile**: Rubio has been the public face of the Trump administration's military strikes on Iran (Operation Epic Fury). He was at Trump's side at Mar-a-Lago during the strikes, while Vance was in Washington at the Situation Room. Trump has called Rubio "the greatest secretary of state in history" and praised him repeatedly at public events.

**Trump's Informal Polling**: At a Mar-a-Lago gathering on Feb 28 with ~25 GOP donors (including Robert Kraft), Trump asked the room "Marco or JD?" -- attendees overwhelmingly cheered for Rubio. One attendee described it as "almost unanimous for Marco." A longtime Trump fundraiser described the current dynamic as "80-20 Marco" in the donor class.

**Rubio Rising on Prediction Markets**: On Kalshi, Rubio leads at 20%, ahead of both Vance and Newsom at 18%. On Polymarket, Rubio has risen to 15% -- nearly triple since December 2025.

### "Draft Rubio" Movement
- A group of Republican megadonors is quietly plotting a "draft Rubio" 2028 effort (ABC News, March 16, 2026)
- The effort is being driven by donors and surrogates, not Rubio himself
- Plans include elevating Rubio within the party and standing up infrastructure after the midterms
- Rubio's public position: "If JD Vance runs for president, he's going to be our nominee, and I'll be one of the first people to support him" (Vanity Fair)

### Strengths
- Foreign policy expertise and highly visible role in Iran crisis
- Trump's increasingly public admiration: "I don't want him to get too popular"
- Growing donor enthusiasm and "Draft Rubio" infrastructure
- Diplomatic persona contrasts with Vance's more combative style
- Deep Florida roots

### Weaknesses
- Iran war is unpopular: 54% of voters disapprove of Trump's handling (NBC)
- Past presidential failure in 2016: lost Florida to Trump, suspended campaign
- Net-negative favorability among all voters (-7 points)
- Rubio's messaging on Iran had hiccups (suggested Israel forced Trump's hand; had to clarify)
- "It's the worst time for Marco to pop" -- former Trump admin official, noting the war's unpopularity

### Key Quote
> "The President has assembled an all-star team... No amount of crazed media speculation about Vice President Vance and Secretary Rubio will deter this Administration's mission." -- White House spokesman Steven Cheung

## 2. Ron DeSantis -- The Diminished Former Frontrunner

![DeSantis](../assets/desantis-01.jpg)

### Current Position
- **Title**: 46th Governor of Florida (term-limited, leaves office Jan 2027)
- **Age**: 46 (born Sept 1978)
- **2028 Status**: Exploratory posturing, but not declared

### Strategic Positioning
DeSantis has been laying groundwork for a potential 2028 comeback despite public denials:
- **Property Tax Elimination**: Pushing a state constitutional amendment to eliminate property taxes for Florida homeowners -- if successful, would give him "serious bragging rights" with the low-tax GOP wing
- **AI Bill of Rights**: Proposed Florida-specific AI governance ahead of Trump's federal AI executive order
- **Redistricting**: Working to redraw Florida's congressional map to add 3-5 more Republican seats
- **Anti-Terrorism Designations**: Declared CAIR and Muslim Brotherhood as terrorist organizations, outpacing the Trump administration
- **U.S. Term Limits**: National co-chairman, maintaining bipartisan visibility

### "Alligator Alcatraz" Controversy
DeSantis's most controversial policy initiative: a massive immigrant detention facility in the Florida Everglades, costing **$1.2 million per day** to operate. Key facts:
- Total cost estimated at $1.065-$1.7 billion
- Florida taxpayers spent over $600 million with uncertain federal reimbursement
- FEMA initially placed an environmental hold on a $608M grant; later lifted
- A Republican House bill blocking new funding for the facility advanced unanimously
- Politico (Feb 3, 2026) reported detailed spending: $573 million on immigration emergency

### Polling & Betting Odds
- RCP Average: 9.0% (4th place)
- Betting odds: only 3% chance of winning GOP nomination (popular betting sites)
- Florida approval: net plus-six, "steady in-state support even as his national profile cools"

### Assessment
> "Although there is no DeSantis 2028 groundswell, he could eventually make a comeback... For the rest of this decade, DeSantis's moment appears 'DeMinished.'" -- The Hill (Aug 2025)

> "His path to the White House is less obvious for 2028 than it was when he ran in 2024." -- Darryl Paulson, USF professor emeritus

DeSantis remains viable if Trump's popularity dims among independents and some GOP voters. His September 2027 departure from the governorship gives him nearly two years to campaign without encumbrances.

## 3. Donald Trump Jr. -- The MAGA Dark Horse

![Trump Jr](../assets/trumpjr-01.jpg)

### Current Position
- **Title**: Executive Vice President, The Trump Organization; Co-Founder of World Liberty Financial
- **Age**: 48 (born Dec 1977)
- **2028 Status**: Not declared, publicly says "ZERO interest" but refuses to rule it out

### Polling Performance
- **RCP Average**: 11.0% (2nd place behind Vance)
- Consistently polls as the second most popular potential 2028 GOP candidate
- November 2025 survey showed him "closing the gap on JD Vance" among Republican voters

### Political Activities
- Maintains massive social media following and influence with the MAGA base
- Was pivotal in elevating Vance to the VP slot in 2024, along with Tucker Carlson
- At Doha Forum (2026), teased the idea of a Trump "third term" in 2028
- CBS News: "maybe one day" he'll run for president
- Newsweek (Feb 2026): "blasted a story with unnamed sources that says he is 'seriously' considering a run"

### Strategic Significance for Vance
Trump Jr. is one of Vance's most important backers. His support for Vance vs. any challenger could be decisive in the primary. He also represents the possibility of a "dynasty" ticket that would preserve the Trump political brand.

### Key Tension
> "The Trump Shadow: Could Donald Jr run in 2028 and keep Trump's political dynasty alive?" -- The Independent

Trump Jr.'s position is unique: he has both the MAGA base's affection and the Trump family name, but has never held elected office and has limited policy depth.

## 4. Nikki Haley -- The Distant Establishment Option

![Haley](../assets/haley-01.jpg)

### Current Position
- **Title**: Former U.S. Ambassador to the UN (2017-2018); Former Governor of South Carolina (2011-2017)
- **Age**: 53 (born Jan 1972)
- **2028 Status**: Possible but unlikely

### Current Standing
- **RCP Average**: 4.5% (5th place)
- Suspended her 2024 campaign after Super Tuesday losses to Trump
- Despite staying in the 2024 race longer than expected, she has not built significant momentum for 2028
- National Review: "Nikki Haley 2028 Is Not Happening"
- The Hill: "GOP party won't want Nikki Haley in 2028"

### Fundraising & Donor Base
- Her 2024 campaign super PAC (SFA Fund) raised significant sums but burned through cash quickly
- She retains relationships with establishment GOP donors who are uncomfortable with MAGA populism
- However, many of those same donors are now gravitating toward Rubio

### 2028 Viability
Haley faces an uphill battle. The party has moved further toward Trumpism since 2024, and her 2024 refusal to endorse Trump quickly alienated the MAGA base. Her polling remains in single digits, and she has no current platform or base of operations to build from.

## 5. Vivek Ramaswamy -- The Ohio Governor Candidate

![Ramaswamy](../assets/ramaswamy-01.jpg)

### Current Position
- **Title**: Republican candidate for Governor of Ohio
- **Age**: 39 (born Aug 1985)
- **Election**: Ohio GOP primary on May 5, 2026; general election Nov 2026
- **2028 Presidential**: Not a factor if focused on Ohio

### Ohio Governor's Race Status
- **Won Ohio Republican Party endorsement** over Ohio AG Dave Yost and entrepreneur Heather Hill
- Announced running mate for the general election
- **Rebranding effort**: CNN (Dec 2025) described him as "conservative without being combative"
- NBC News: "The Vivek Ramaswamy running for governor of Ohio in 2026 is different from the one you might remember running for president in 2024"
- Launched a **$10 million statewide ad campaign**
- Ohio governor's race set to become "most expensive in state history"

### Polling Headwinds
- Newsweek: Ramaswamy "dealt double polling blow" -- trailing Democrat Amy Acton in the gubernatorial race
- NYT: Race "widely seen as competitive"
- His presidential campaign persona (combative, ideological) is being softened for the governor's race

### Relationship to Vance
- Briefly co-headed DOGE with Elon Musk before resigning to run for Ohio governor
- Shares ideological alignment with Vance on many issues but they operate in different spheres
- An Ohio governorship would position him as a potential 2032 or 2036 presidential candidate, not 2028

## 6. Byron Donalds -- The Florida Governor Frontrunner

![Donalds](../assets/donalds-01.jpg)

### Current Position
- **Title**: U.S. Representative for Florida's 19th District (2021-present)
- **Age**: 47 (born Oct 1978)
- **Election**: 2026 Florida gubernatorial race
- **2028 Presidential**: Not a factor; focused on Florida

### Florida Governor's Race Status
- **Trump-endorsed** candidate for governor
- **Fundraising**: Raised over $40 million since launch; $45 million over 2025; $13 million in Q4 2025 alone
- **Polling**: Dominant lead in GOP primary -- one survey showed him "blowing away the rest of the field"
- UNF Poll: 4-point lead over Casey DeSantis among likely Republican primary voters
- Described by some as the "most dominant" fundraising start for a Florida governor's campaign ever
- Main Republican rivals: Casey DeSantis (wife of current governor), former Florida House Speaker Paul Renner, Lt. Gov. Jay Collins

### Strategic Position
If elected Florida governor, Donalds would become a potential 2032+ presidential candidate. He represents the next generation of Florida Republicans and has strong MAGA credentials. His 2026 race is effectively a proxy battle between Trump's endorsement (Donalds) and the DeSantis political machine (Casey DeSantis).

## 8. Key Dynamics Shaping the Race

### The "Tucker Dilemma" for Vance
Laura Loomer, the influential far-right activist with Trump's ear, has emerged as one of Vance's staunchest critics from within the MAGA base. She has called on Vance to condemn Tucker Carlson for his criticism of the Iran strikes. Her message: "If he doesn't disavow [Tucker], Marco's going to be the nominee." This represents a significant intra-MAGA fault line.

### Trump's Kingmaker Role
- Trump has said he would "be inclined" to endorse a successor in the primaries
- He has praised both Vance ("brilliant") and Rubio ("best secretary of state in history")
- He has teased a "dream ticket" of Vance-Rubio
- His inner circle reports he "talks about Marco and not JD" more recently
- **However**: Longtime Trump advisers caution that no final answer is imminent -- "This is just what Trump does"

### The Iran War as Wild Card
- The war is overwhelmingly unpopular: 54% disapprove of Trump's handling (NBC)
- Only 29% approve of the strikes (Ipsos)
- 90% of MAGA-aligned Republicans supported the strikes
- Rubio is tied to the war as Secretary of State; Vance opposed military action internally before supporting it publicly
- This could cut either way for both candidates

### Donor Class vs. Base
> "Donors don't pick the nominee -- the base picks. Donors tried to abandon President Trump and tried to pick DeSantis, and we all saw how that went." -- Senior Republican operative to ABC News

The core tension: Vance has the base (49% in RCP average), but Rubio has the donor enthusiasm and the current moment of foreign policy visibility.
