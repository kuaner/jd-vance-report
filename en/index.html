A dissection of power — from a Rust Belt youth to the Vice Presidency of the United States

Between the hedgehog and the fox, there are those whose changes are not evolution but adaptation. They are not seeking truth — they are seeking patrons. Each shedding of skin leaves them smoother, and harder to hold.

Adapted from Isaiah Berlin, The Hedgehog and the Fox

In 2016, he called Trump "America's Hitler." In 2025, he stood beside Trump as Vice President. In nine years, he changed his name, his religion, and his stance on virtually every major political issue.

J.D. Vance is one of the most bewildering figures in American politics.

Behind these transformations lies no deep conviction — only a meticulously calculated ascent to power. From the Ohio Rust Belt to Yale Law School, from Silicon Valley venture capital to the United States Senate, at every stop someone opened the door: Peter Thiel invested capital in him, David Sacks leveraged connections on his behalf, Donald Trump Jr. cleared the political path for him, and Elon Musk deployed the megaphone to amplify him.

He is not a politician in the traditional sense. He is a manufactured product — Silicon Valley's tech elite needed a voice in Washington, and a veteran from the Rust Belt fit every requirement: a narrative of suffering, academic credentials, a military record, and a hillbilly story that resonates with white working-class voters.

This report traces every critical node on the path from Middletown to the White House. It is not a biography — it is a systematic dissection, attempting to answer a deeper question: in post-Trump America, how is the transmission of power changing?
